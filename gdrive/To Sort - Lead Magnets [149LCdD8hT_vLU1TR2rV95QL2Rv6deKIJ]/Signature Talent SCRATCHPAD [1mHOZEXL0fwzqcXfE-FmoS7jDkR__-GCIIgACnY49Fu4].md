# **Signature Talent**

## How the Highest-Performing Business Leaders Get the Most Done with the Least Effort

When I was in the 7th grade, I sat at my desk in a small outbuilding on the school property. The rest of the students hunched over their books. The only sound was the sound of scratching pencils.

A piece of narrow-ruled notebook paper sat in front of me. I held a eversharp pencil in my hand.

And my mind was 1700 miles away.

“What would it be like to be like Bill Gates?”

It’s not that I wanted his extravagant mansion, or even that I wanted my name on a Forbes Billionaires list.

It was the first time in my life that I clearly remember the sensation of ease and calm that came with something I had yet to put words to.

It would take me years to come back to this moment and discover what that sense actually pointed to.

## The Destructive Myth of Effort and Time

When I graduated from high school, I began my career in software development. As time continued, I lost the thrill of the chase. Work became routine. I began forcing myself to show up to work each morning.

Because I wasn’t engaged, I wasn’t given more opportunity in my role. And because I didn’t have opportunities for growth, I never became more engaged.

When I had finally exhausted nearly all of my efforts, I finally had a slow but forceful realization.

**Effort and time do not guarantee my success**.

As this realization began to sink in, I realized I had to dig deeper to find answers. I had to get to the root of what’s going on.

The answer shocked me.
