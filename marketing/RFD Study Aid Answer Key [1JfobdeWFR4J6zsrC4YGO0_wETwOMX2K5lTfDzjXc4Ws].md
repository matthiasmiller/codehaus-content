Page 1- **every level, vision statement, job description, result, task, fuzzies**

Clear outcomes must exist at **every level** of the organization, from the **vision statement** to the **job description***.*

Does this focus on the **result** instead of the **task**?  
Does this have any **fuzzies**?

Page 2 \- **result, how, aligning, work, knowledge, skill, personality, instinctive behavior**

Focusing on the **result** encourages innovation. When you stop focusing on the **“how”**, you give your team members the opportunity to find new ways to achieve better results.

Leadership is about **aligning** people with the **work** that needs to be done.

Cognitive \- Do they have the **knowledge** and **skill** required to to do the job, or can we help them gain it?

Affective \- Do they have the **personality** required to do the job?

Conative \- Does their **instinctive behavior** fit the job?

Page 3 \- **steal, ill, vacation, leaves, mistakes**

Character \- Under what conditions will this individual **steal** from you?

When a team member is unexpectedly **ill** or on **vacation**  
When a team member **leaves** the organization  
When a team member makes frequent, varying **mistakes**

Page 4 \- **mindset, process, accountability, follow, change, incompetence, processes, systems, done, right person, resources, tools, timeline**

A critical **mindset** is understanding that everything you do is a **process**.

They must simple, built-in accountability.  
They must be easy for another team member to follow.  
They must be easy to change.

Outstanding team members do not fail because of **incompetence**. They fail because they lack the support of proper **processes**, and they lack the **systems** they need to continue using them.

Clarify the specific definition of **done**  
Identify the **person** for it.  
Collect the **resources** or **tools** needed for it.  
Communicate and negotiate the **timeline**.

Page 5 \- **focus, time, focus, energy, story, someone else, assumptions, win, delegate**

Find a Place to **Focus**  
What are the things taking up significant amounts of **time**?  
What are the things taking up significant amounts of **focus**?  
What are the things taking up significant amounts of **energy**?

Tell the **Story** of What Needs to be Done  
It’s often easier to have **someone else** help you, because they can uncover your **assumptions**.

Build the Tools to Help Them **Win**

Identify Things that You Can **Delegate**

Page 6 \- **too much, top-down, organizational processes, tools, processes, grow, scale, too late, whirlwind**

1. Doing **too much** at once  
   2. Implementing from the **top-down**  
   3. Hiring a coach who’s only an expert on **organizational processes**  
   4. Hiring a consultant who understands **tools** but not **processes**  
   5. Choosing tools That won’t **grow** and **scale** with you  
   6. Thinking about processes after it’s **too late**  
   7. Letting the **whirlwind** take control of your future
