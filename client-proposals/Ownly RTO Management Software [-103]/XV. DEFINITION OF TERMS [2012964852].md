This glossary provides a reference for key terms and their definitions used in the Ownly RTO Management Software.

  


RTO: Rent-to-Own, a contract allowing purchase of a product through periodic payments. Refer to state legislation governing this product, as it differs from traditional financing or credit sales.

  


RTR: Rent-to-Rent, this is strictly a rental product for such things as storage units or equipment.

  


RTO Terms: The duration of a rent-to-own (RTO) contract, typically 24, 36, 48, or 60 months.

  


Repayment Cycle: The frequency at which a customer repays their RTO or RTR contract (e.g., Monthly, Bi-Weekly, Weekly, Semi-Monthly).

  


Early Cash Payoff (EPO): The amount a customer can pay to settle their RTO contract before the full term.

  


Cash Reserve Account (CRA): An optional RTO contract feature, allowed in most states, where an initial lump-sum payment reduces monthly payments.

  


Security Deposit: An upfront payment that does not affect monthly payments, used for repairs, past-due rent, or applied at contract payoff.

  


Balloon Payment: A larger final payment required in some RTO contracts, notably in states like North Carolina without specific RTO legislation.

  


Rental Company: The entity that purchases and manages RTO contracts for customers.

  


Contract Definition: A template specifying contract parameters (e.g., fees, terms) for a Rental Company in a specific state.

  


Rental Contract Type: Specifies whether a contract is Rent-to-Own (RTO) or Rent-to-Rent (RTR).

  


Damage Waiver Fee (DWF): An optional fee in RTO and RTR contracts, paid per payment period, that absolves the customer of responsibility for product damage or loss.

  


Late Fee: A charge for overdue payments, with configurable grace periods and amounts.

  


Convenience Fee: An additional charge for specific payment methods (e.g., credit card, ACH), subject to state regulations. This feature will only be available by special request and signing of a waiver.

  


Note Type: Categories for notes linked to records (e.g., Customer Task, Letter Template, Call Script).

  


Note Template: Predefined templates for communications or exports, used within the note system.

  


Promised Payment: A recorded commitment for a future payment, including a specified amount and follow-up date.

  


Depreciation: The reduction in asset value over time, tracked using book and tax models (e.g., Straight-Line, Accelerated).

  


Manufacturer: An organization that produces products (e.g., sheds) for Rental Companies.

  


Dealer: An organization that distributes or resells products, linked to Manufacturers and Salespersons.

  


Salesperson: An individual linked to a Dealer, responsible for sales activities.

  


Contact Type: Categorizes contacts in the system (e.g., Customer, Rental Company, Manufacturer, Salesperson).

  


Product: A physical item (e.g., a building) tracked in the system, with details like serial number and depreciation.

  


Detail Screen: The screen that visualizes the information of a specific record.

  


RG: Repeating Group, also called a mini spreadsheet. These can be found on detail screens and are used to group information that have repeated values.

  


RTF: Rich Text Field, mostly found in use as notes sections that allow you to do simple formatting of text.
