2.1. Glossary

Overview: The purpose of this glossary is to provide a description of key terms, roles, and other things referred to in this proposal. These are listed in alphabetical order below: 

  


  * Agent: A person responsible to manage coverage, vehicle information, claims, invoices, etc for their clients. Agents are tracked on Contact records in the Solution, and may also be clients themselves.
  * Claim: An official record of an accident or incident involving a vehicle in the Plan. These are tracked on Claim records in the Solution. 
  * Client: A person who has at least one active vehicle being covered by the Plan. Also referred to as a "Member" or Vehicle "Owner". Clients are tracked on Contact records in the Solution. 
  * Coverages: 
    * Cargo coverage: Coverage to cover the cost of loss of cargo carried by the covered vehicle.
    * Collision coverage: The generic / underlying terminology for the more advanced / complete coverage option offered by the Plan. Referred to as "Vehicle Loss Sharing" or "VLS" visually throughout the Solution, based on a System Switch setting. 
    * Liability coverage: This is the most basic coverage that the Plan offers. 
  * Coverage Fee Types: 
    * Entry Fee: A fee charged to a vehicle owner when they add a new coverage to a vehicle. 
    * Premium: Annual fee charged when the coverage is activated or renewed.
  * High-Risk Driver Fee: An extra fee charged for drivers that pose a higher risk of incident.
  * Period Year: Default length of time for coverage. 
    * Period Start Date: Always is July 1.  
    * Period End Date: Always is June 30. 
  * SI #: This is the "Self Insured" policy number. It may be a single number the applies to the entire Plan, or a separate number for each individual client (determined by a System Switch setting). 
  * Vehicle Class: The categorization of vehicles based on their type, size, and weight. A vehicle with a higher number of Classes will have a higher Liability Premium.
  * Vehicle Loss Sharing (or "VLS"): Alternate terminology for "Collision" coverage.
